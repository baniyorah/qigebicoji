Miles Brown Biography

Miles Brown is an American actor and dancer who is also known by his stage name, Baby Boogaloo. In his acting career, Miles is most known as Jack Johnson, his role in the ABC comedy series Black-ish. He is the youngest member of the Jr. NBA Leadership Council.

Miles began dancing at the age of three and got to dance in music videos like “Yeah 3x” and “Loving You Is Killing Me” as well as on Yo Gabba Gabba!, a children’s show. When he was five, he became a guest on the Ellen DeGeneres show and a contestant on the fifth season of America’s Got Talent as part of the dance duo “Future Funk.”

Then came his first feature film role as part of the Little Rascals Intro Battle Crew in the dance-drama movie Battlefield America.

    Miles’ Father and Miles’ Mom

    Sister

    Miles has an older sister by the name Kiana Brown. On set, he has a fraternal twin sister, Diane Johnson, the character portrayed by Marsai Martin. 

    Miles Brown Black-ish

    In 2014, Brown became a part of the cast of Black-ish, a 2014 sitcom TV series created by Kenya Barris. He is cast as Jack Johnson with a twin by the name Diane Johnson opposite actor Anthony Anderson and actress Tracee Ellis Ross as their parents.

    For his role as on the show, he has been nominated for four NAACP Image Awards, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, and the BET YoungStars Award and won a Young Artist Award for Best Performance in a TV Series – Supporting Young Actor.

In the semi-finals, he teamed up with actress Mackenzie Ziegler and they received a perfect score from the judge on 2nd December 2018. His dancing skills have led him to be regarded as an uncanny Michael Jackson.
